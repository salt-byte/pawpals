# Building a Promotion Case

## What This Covers
- Promotion
- Getting promoted
- Leveling up
- Career ladder
- Advancement

## Top Insights

### From Claire Vo
*Claire Vo*

> "know what you want out of your career, be clear and ask for it, and then make it easy for your boss or whoever can support or champion you to get you from here to there."

**Key insight:** Career advancement requires clarity of goals and making the promotion an easy decision for leadership by solving organizational problems.

**Apply this by:**
- Define exactly what you want your next role to be
- Align your promotion case with solving a specific company problem
- Understand the talent calendar and promotion cycles of your organization

### From Ethan Evans
*Ethan Evans 2.0*

> "The basic idea of The Magic Loop is five steps and they're very easy. The first one is you have to be doing your current job well... Then step two is ask your boss how you can help... Step three is whatever they say, do it... the fourth step is where the magic comes in. You go back to your manager..."

**Key insight:** The Magic Loop is a five-step partnership framework designed to align your work with your manager's needs to secure promotions and career growth.

**Apply this by:**
- Ensure you are performing at a solid level before asking for more
- Proactively ask your manager 'What can I do to help you?'
- Execute on the manager's requests, even if they are maintenance tasks, to build trust

### From Jeffrey Pfeffer
*Jeffrey Pfeffer*

> "The reason why you should pay attention to this is because it leads to a lot of good things, salary, getting promoted, being happy in your career, being less stressed."

**Key insight:** Political and power skills are directly correlated with tangible career outcomes like salary increases and promotions.

**Apply this by:**
- Master political skills to improve your chances of advancement and higher compensation.

### From Nikhyl Singhal
*Nikhyl Singhal*

> "I think that the biggest one in workplace is focusing career and promotion together. I think that there's perhaps a light connection between promotion and career addition, but I feel like too many people are, the moment we talk about career, they're like, 'Well, let me talk to you. I want to have a..."

**Key insight:** Promotion is a company-specific system for leveling, whereas a career is a long-term personal arc; don't conflate the two.

**Apply this by:**
- Distinguish between 'promotion talks' (short-term leveling) and 'career talks' (long-term growth)
- Evaluate if a promotion is actually in service of your 'job after next'

### From Tamar Yehoshua
*Tamar Yehoshua*

> "One of the things that I think is overlooked is do a really good job at whatever your job is at that point. People have a tendency ... Especially product managers are very ambitious and they want to get to the next level and they're always eyeing the next job, but you're not going to get the next..."

**Key insight:** The most effective way to advance is to achieve excellence in your current role rather than focusing prematurely on the next one.

**Apply this by:**
- Master the 'table stakes' of your current role: technical knowledge, product understanding, and metrics.
